Projects

Projects that highlight my interests.

Sign Language

My students love learning American Sign Language (ASL) from me. They realize that ASL is not only for the deaf and hard of hearing community, but also for others who are curious. Sign language is a great skill to learn and it also helps with classroom management.

Pen Pal Exchanges

By engaging my students in pen pal exchanges in Taiwan, Kenya, and other parts of the USA, I broaden my students' understanding of the world beyond their neighborhood community. I want my students to recognize that while we celebrate differences amongst us, there are many experiences, languages, and identities that also connect us to others around the world.

Mindfulness Meditation

Breathe in, breathe out. I teach mindfulness to my students to help them recognize how they are feeling and bring attention to the present moment. With the hustle and bustle of the day, slowing down and taking deep breaths help center and calm all of us.

Teaching Code and Learning to Code

I not only teach my students computer science, but I am also learning how to code html/css/javascript as a MotherCoder fellow. I am passionate about bringing computer science to my students and also coding when I have a free moment.

Tools

Various tools I use in my classroom.

Universal Design for Learning Tool

I follow universal design for learning (UDL) guidelines outlined by CAST. UDL as a framework helps all students, not just special needs students or English Language Learners.

Station Tool

In my first foray into coding, I designed a tool for my classroom specifically for when I teach using the stations model. In the stations model, my class is divided into 3 groups for 3 stations. Each group rotates to each station after a set amount of time. Some students can get confused where they go next and what they need to bring to the next station. I developed this visual tool to help all students know where to go and what to bring.

Digital Portfolios

While I am working to develop my own digital portfolios to showcase the work my students have done, I currently use Seesaw to create interactive lessons and to show and share my students work with each other. Seesaw is a great digital learning tool for students and teachers.
